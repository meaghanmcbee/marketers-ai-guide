The reality check

The gap is closing. Fast.

You've built your career on great instincts, strong writing, and knowing your audience. Good news: none of that goes away. The marketers winning with AI aren't the ones who handed over the wheel — they're the ones who know exactly which tasks to delegate, and which ones need a human at the helm.

From Anthropic's own guidance on using AI well AI works best when you already know what "good" looks like. Before you prompt, know the output you want. You are the editor. Always.
1

Know what good looks like first

If you can't spot a bad output, you can't improve a good one. Your experience is your competitive advantage over someone who just started using AI with no marketing background.

2

Start with the right use cases

Not everything belongs in an AI prompt. The sweet spot is high-volume, repeatable tasks where you can verify quality quickly: first drafts, data summaries, ideation, repurposing.

3

Treat it like a very capable intern

Give it context. Give it examples. Tell it the format you want, the audience, the tone. Vague in = vague out. Specific in = specific out.

4

Never ship without editing

AI can hallucinate facts. It doesn't know your brand voice until you teach it. Every output is a draft — your name is on the final version.

Process templates

Build Your AI Workflow

A prompt is a one-time shortcut. A workflow is how you build habits that compound. These are repeatable processes — not just prompts — for the most common marketing tasks.

Blog Production

The 4-Step Blog Workflow

1

Brief the AI (Claude)

Give it the keyword, audience, tone, and structure. Get an outline back. Edit the outline before writing begins — this saves time later.

2

Generate section by section

Don't ask for the whole post at once. Prompt each H2 section individually. This gives you more control and better quality per section.

3

Add your expert layer

Insert real examples, opinions, data, and brand voice. This is where experience years matters — AI can't fake lived experience.

4

Final edit pass

Read it out loud. Does it sound like you? Check all facts. Remove any claims that feel invented. Add internal links.

Monthly Reporting

The Reporting Workflow

1

Export your data first

Get everything in one place before prompting. AI can't pull data — you need to bring the numbers to it.

2

Paste & summarize (Claude/Copilot)

Use the analytics summary prompt from the Prompts section. Give context on what your goals were this month.

3

Build the narrative, not just the data

Ask AI to write the "so what" — the story behind the numbers. Executives want insight, not spreadsheets.

4

Review & add context

You know why Q3 dipped. AI doesn't. Add that context manually — it's the difference between a mediocre report and a great one.

Campaign Launch

Campaign Copy Workflow

1

Write the brief yourself first

Before prompting for copy, write the campaign brief. The quality of your brief is directly proportional to the quality of AI output.

2

Generate hero messaging (Claude)

Start with the headline and core value proposition. Get 5 options. Pick the strongest angle, then build everything else from it.

3

Cascade to channels (ChatGPT)

Take your approved hero message and adapt it for each channel: email, social, ads, landing page. Maintain the core message, adjust the format.

4

QA everything as a batch

Paste all your final copy variants into Claude and ask it to flag any inconsistencies in messaging, tone, or factual claims across all pieces.

SEO Strategy

Content Gap Research

1

Define your topic cluster

Give AI your primary topic and ask it to map out 20 related subtopics across the buyer journey (awareness → consideration → decision).

2

Compare against what you have

List your existing content. Ask AI to identify gaps and prioritize by likely search intent and audience readiness.

3

Brief the top 5 gaps

Use the SEO brief template for each. Get briefs in 30 minutes instead of half a day.

4

Validate with real search data

AI estimates intent — always check actual search volumes in your SEO tool before committing resources. AI is the starting point, not the source of truth.

The golden rule of AI workflows

Garbage in. Garbage out.

The single biggest predictor of AI output quality is input quality. Marketers who get bad results from AI almost always gave it too little context, no audience information, and no examples of what good looks like. Before you blame the tool, improve the brief. Every time. Without exception.

Content Marketing

How to Build a Full Content Machine Using AI (Without Losing Your Voice)

For: Content marketers | Tools: Claude, ChatGPT | Estimated time saved: 3-5 hours/week

The promise of AI for content marketing is real — but only if you build the right system around it. The marketers who get the best results treat AI like a very capable content team member, not a vending machine you put prompts into and pull content out of.

The 5 stages where AI helps most

1. Ideation & editorial planning +
Use AI to generate 20 content ideas from a single seed topic — then filter with your editorial judgment. This is AI at its best: high volume, low stakes, you pick the winners. Ask ChatGPT for 20 blog ideas around your content pillar, then run the top 5 through Claude for a proper brief. Takes 15 minutes instead of a half-day brainstorm.
2. Research synthesis +
Paste in multiple source documents, reports, or research notes and ask AI to synthesize the key points. This works especially well for topic backgrounders before a briefing call. Always check facts — AI synthesizes well but occasionally misremembers details. Your editing pass catches this.
3. First drafts (section by section) +
Don't ask for the full post at once. Prompt section by section, starting with the outline. This gives you checkpoints to course-correct early rather than editing a 1,500-word draft that went in the wrong direction at paragraph 3. Claude is particularly strong here because it follows structural instructions precisely.
4. The expert layer (you, not AI) +
This is where your 10+ years of marketing experience becomes irreplaceable. After the draft: add your real opinions, insert examples from your actual work or industry, include specific data you know to be true. AI wrote the skeleton. You provide the soul. This is also what protects against the generic AI voice that audiences are increasingly good at spotting.
5. Publishing-ready editing +
One final prompt that earns its keep: paste your full draft and ask AI to flag any unsupported claims, clichés, or areas where the logic doesn't flow. It's like having a developmental editor on call. Then do your own read-aloud pass. If you stumble on a sentence, rewrite it.

Brand Marketing

Protecting Your Brand Voice When Using AI: A Non-Negotiable Guide

For: Brand marketers, content leads | Tools: Claude (recommended) | Priority: High

The fastest way to erode brand equity in the AI era is to let AI write copy without a strong brand voice brief. When you don't tell AI what your brand sounds like, it defaults to a suspiciously pleasant, surprisingly generic, and unmistakably artificial voice. Here's how to prevent that.

Build your AI brand voice brief

Before you write a single marketing prompt for AI, create a brand voice reference document. Paste it at the start of every session, or save it as a custom instruction. Include:

Brand Voice Prompt Prefix
Before we begin: here is our brand voice guide.

Our brand is: [company name], a [describe company].
Our audience is: [describe]
Our tone is: [3-5 adjectives, e.g., direct, warm, expert-but-not-arrogant]
We always: [list 3 things you consistently do, e.g., use active voice, speak to the reader as an equal]
We never: [list words and phrases that are off-brand, e.g., leverage, synergy, game-changing, in today's world]
Sample sentence that sounds like us: [paste an example from existing copy]

With this context in mind, please complete the following task:

This single habit will improve your AI output quality more than any other. Claude in particular responds extremely well to detailed brand voice instructions and will maintain them consistently across a long session.
